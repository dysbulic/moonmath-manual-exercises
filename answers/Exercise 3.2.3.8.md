## *Exercise 3.2.3.8:* (Extended Euclidean Algorithm).

Find integers $s, t \in \mathbb{Z}$ such that $gcd(a, b) = s · a + t · b$ holds for the following pairs:

1. $(a, b) = (45, 10)$
2. $(a, b) = (13, 11)$
3. $(a, b) = (13, 12)$

---
---

1. $(a, b) = (45, 10)$

$gcd = 5$
$5 = (-1) • 45 + (5) • 10$
$s = -1$
$t = 5$

2. $(a, b) = (13, 11)$

$gcd = 1$
$5 = (-5) • 13 + (6) • 11$
$s = -5$
$t = 11$

3. $(a, b) = (13, 12)$

$gcd = 1$
$5 = (1) • 13 + (-1) • 12$
$s = 1$
$t = -1$
